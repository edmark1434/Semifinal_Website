Moalboal HISTORICAL SITES

Historical in Moalboal

"SIMALA" is one of the historic in Moalboal, a charming coastal town in Cebu, Philippines, is not only known for its stunning beaches and vibrant marine life but also for its historical and cultural landmarks. These sites reflect the rich heritage and deep-rooted traditions of the area, offering visitors a glimpse into its storied past.

One of the notable historical landmarks in Moalboal is St. John Nepomucene Parish Church, established in 1852. This Spanish-era church stands as a testament to the enduring Catholic faith of the locals and features classic colonial architecture, with its thick coral stone walls and intricate details. The church is a serene place for reflection and an essential stop for history enthusiasts.

Simala, located in the town of Sibonga, Cebu, is home to the famous Simala Shrine, a significant religious and historical landmark in the province. The Shrine of Our Lady of Lindogon is a majestic castle-like church that has become a pilgrimage site for Catholics seeking spiritual blessings.
