Moalboal FESTIVALS

"SINULOG" & "KAGASANGAN" FESTIVALS

The “KAGASANGAN FESTIVAL” secured seventh place in the “Best Festival” category for Pasigarbo 2023, while its Festival Queen, Michelle Oppliger, finished fourth as Pasigarbo Festival Queen. Moalboal Mayor Inocentes “Titing” Cabaron expressed in his introductory speech for the “Kagasangan Festival” that the town is blessed with many natural resources, from the wind and fresh air to the bodies of water, the sea, and the rich agricultural soil. This well-endowed town has become a major tourist attraction in the region due to its famous beaches and rich marine life.

The "SINULOG FESTIVAL" is a vibrant celebration of Cebuano culture and religious devotion to the Sto. Niño. The Sinulog Festival in Moalboal is a vibrant celebration that honors the Santo Niño (Child Jesus) through colorful parades, traditional dances, and religious rituals. Held annually in January, the festival draws locals and tourists alike, featuring the signature "Sinulog" dance, which mimics the movement of the river's current. Participants wear bright, intricate costumes, and the streets are filled with music, drum beats, and joyous celebrations. The festival not only showcases Moalboal's rich cultural heritage but also highlights the deep religious devotion of its people. It’s a unique fusion of faith, tradition, and community spirit that captivates all who attend.
